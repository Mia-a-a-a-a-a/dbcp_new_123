About us

The Data Buoy Cooperation Panel (DBCP) is one of the observing networks contributing to the Global Ocean Observing System (GOOS) - an international programme providing leadership and strategic framework for observing the ocean in support of climate forecasting, operational services and ocean health.

Co-sponsored by the UNESCOâ€™s Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission (UNESCO-IOC), the World Meteorological Organization (WMO), the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), and the International Science Council (ISC), GOOS integrates a global network of national observing systems, expert panels, regional alliances, and data providers to enable informed decisions for a sustainable ocean and thriving communities worldwide.

The GOOS observing networks provide long-term, global, high-quality, in-situ observations of Essential Ocean Variables - the key measurements needed to assess ocean state and variability. The real-time visualizations of all network platforms and their metadata can be accessed through OceanOPS - the operational coordination and monitoring centre of GOOS.

Mission

Supporting Meteorologists and Oceanographers Worldwide

Oversees a network of over 1,250 drifting buoys and 400 moored buoys, the DBCP addresses the growing need for accurate and timely atmospheric and oceanographic data. Its work supports better global forecasts of weather and ocean conditions while contributing to important climate studies and oceanographic research. rovides essential measurements, including sea-surface temperature, surface current velocity, air temperature and wind speed and direction. These observations are critical for improving weather and ocean forecasts, scientific research, and operational models.

One of the focus of the DBCP is identifying and addressing data needs. The panel reviews and evaluates evolving requirements for buoy data to meet the needs of the global community. It works on facilitating deployment and expanding the network, to coordinate and support deployment programs, ensuring the network meets operational and scientific demands. In promoting collaboration and technology sharing, DBCP encourages communication and supports technological advancements, enables the exchange of best practices and innovations. The panel works in improving data quality and accessibility to enhance the availability, quality, and timeliness of buoy data shared. In fostering collaborative efforts, the Panel brings together diverse stakeholders to address common goals through action groups and partnerships. And by engaging with key organizations, the panel works with international and national bodies to harmonize efforts and amplify the impact of its initiatives.

The DBCP operates under a clear framework of strategic aims and activities, which are designed to address the critical needs of the oceanographic and meteorological communities. These aims ensure the effective coordination of resources, the advancement of technologies, and the sharing of valuable data. Together, they guide the panel's efforts to support global observational and forecasting systems:

  1. Reviewing and analyzing the evolving needs for buoy data.
  2. Coordinating deployment programs to align with the goals of the Global Ocean Observing System (GOOS).
  3. Facilitating the exchange of information and development of new technologies.
  4. Enhancing the distribution of buoy data through the Global Telecommunication System (GTS) and WIS2.0.
  5. Supporting action groups to address specific regional or technical challenges.
  6. Building partnerships with organizations worldwide to achieve shared objectives.

Membership in the DBCP is open to IOC and WMO member states and other interested parties, emphasizing the importance of collective action in addressing global challenges. By bringing together nations, organizations, and experts, the DBCP provides a platform for progress in the global ocean and atmospheric observation community.

Governance

The panel is led by a Chair and Vice Chair representing participating continents and is supported by then JCOMM secretariat, which since 2020 the Joint WMO-IOC Collaborative Board rebranded into OceanOPS. An international group of members convenes annually to review progress, address challenges, and set future priorities. The Technical Coordinator, based at OceanOPS, plays a vital role in supporting the panelâ€™s efforts. The DBCP also organizes annual Science and Technology Workshops to encourage collaboration and knowledge sharing among experts.

Action Groups

The Data Buoy Cooperation Panel (DBCP) Action Groups play a pivotal role in advancing global ocean observation by overseeing independent buoy programs that deliver critical meteorological and oceanographic data. These groups, formally approved by the DBCP, support vital programs like the World Weather Watch and the Global Ocean Observing System, ensuring timely and high-quality data reaches users worldwide. By fostering collaboration and technology exchange, DBCP Action Groups strengthen the international community's ability to monitor and understand our oceans, driving impactful research and environmental stewardship.

The DBCP operates through a variety of teams and groups, including:

  • An Executive Board: Oversees strategic planning and decision-making.
  • Action Groups: Focused teams addressing specific regional or operational needs.
  • Task Teams: Dedicated to tackling technical challenges and advancing technology development.
